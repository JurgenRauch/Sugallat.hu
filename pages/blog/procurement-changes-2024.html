Public Procurement Changes in 2024: What to Expect?

The new year has brought significant changes to public procurement procedures. We summarize the most important modifications and their practical implications.

The year 2024 marks an important milestone in the development of public procurement procedures, with several significant legislative changes coming into effect. These modifications affect both contracting authorities and economic operators, requiring renewed attention to compliance and procedural requirements.

Key Changes in Legislation

The most significant changes include updated threshold values, modified evaluation criteria, and enhanced transparency requirements. These adjustments aim to create a more competitive and fair procurement environment while ensuring better value for public money.

Impact on Contracting Authorities

Contracting authorities must now adapt their procurement strategies to accommodate new requirements for documentation, evaluation processes, and supplier engagement. This includes updated templates for procurement documents and revised procedures for handling complaints and appeals.

Implications for Economic Operators

Suppliers and contractors should be aware of the new qualification requirements and evaluation methodologies. Understanding these changes is crucial for successful bid preparation and maintaining competitiveness in the public procurement market.
