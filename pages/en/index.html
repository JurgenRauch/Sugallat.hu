The Public Procurement Act in Practice

The regulatory framework of the Public Procurement Act often requires specialised interpretation, particularly in complex projects. It is not enough to know the law; one must also be able to apply it and enforce one's rights. Our experience ensures that public procurement rules are effectively implemented in day-to-day operations.

Information and Questions

Public procurement is mandatory when the purchasing organisation qualifies as a contracting authority and the value of the procurement reaches the threshold defined by law, known as the public procurement threshold. A procurement expert continuously monitors legislative changes and informs clients about threshold adjustments as well as new requirements in the procurement market.

The consultant's role is to provide professional support throughout the entire procedure, from planning to completion, ensuring regulatory compliance and efficient management so that the process is secure and successful in every respect.

For certain procedure types and thresholds, the law requires the involvement of an accredited consultant, but it is also advisable for complex projects even when not mandatory.

The in-house officer is an internal staff member of the organisation, while the consultant supports the procedure as an external, independent expert. The two roles complement each other; the cooperation between the internal officer and the external consultant is the most effective solution.

The EKR (Electronic Public Procurement System) provides fully electronic management of public procurement procedures, increasing transparency and legal certainty, although its effective use often requires professional expertise.

Conducting Public Procurement Procedures

The full-service management of public procurement procedures is a complex process from preparation to contract award, where every step is aligned with current legislation and professional requirements. During the management of procurement procedures, legal, professional, and administrative aspects all receive high priority.

A well-structured and transparent process supports responsible decision-making, increases the effectiveness of the procedure, and creates a stable foundation for the successful implementation of projects in the long term.

The preparation of a public procurement procedure includes the compilation of documentation and management of necessary clarifications through to contract award. Professional coordination is required throughout procurement procedures, while the precise conduct of every public procurement procedure is critical to success.
