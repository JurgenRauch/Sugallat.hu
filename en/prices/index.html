What influences the price?

The level of fees depends on several factors based on the applicable official pricing framework. Procedures reaching EU thresholds require more documentation and longer evaluation periods, resulting in higher amounts. In the case of negotiated procedures, iterative consultations increase the expert working time as part of the procedure outcome. When determining prices, the complexity of technical documentation and the sophistication of the evaluation criteria must also be taken into account. For complex procurements, the higher amount reflects the time required for more detailed preparation. The lower percentage fee for construction projects stems from standardised technical documentation, while the higher prices for supply and services cover the additional work involved in developing constantly changing specifications.

Estimated value of procurement

The total value of a public procurement procedure represents the expected consideration corresponding to the estimated value of the given procurement, which determines the applicable procedure and thresholds. Without an accurate estimate, the procurement process cannot be lawfully planned and conducted within a public procurement procedure.

Through various procurement procedures, a documented estimate reduces the margin for error, allowing the contracting authority to plan its costs and unforeseen expenses more accurately. This provides more transparent planning for all parties involved.

EU and Hungarian procurement thresholds

The EU thresholds and national (Public Procurement Act) procurement limits determine which procedure types may be applied for the contracting authority's given procurement. These threshold values are regulated by the Public Procurement Act and EU directives, which define the procedural rules for procurements reaching EU thresholds, and are regularly updated when new provisions come into force.

Frequently Asked Questions

Consulting fees are determined by the nature of the project: a simple service procedure requires lower, while a complex construction procurement requires higher expert input. Our goal is to ensure that the investment is always proportional to the results achievable through contract award.

The costs of a public procurement procedure may include consulting fees, legal representation fees related to potential remedy proceedings, as well as administrative and publication fees. A well-structured process reduces unforeseen expenses.

The cost of construction procurement always depends on the value and complexity of the given construction project. For a simple procedure, the expenditure is lower, while a high-value project or framework agreement contracts require more resources, including professional support for the tender documentation.
