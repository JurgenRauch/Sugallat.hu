Dispute Resolution and Remedy Consulting

Content and Formal Requirements of a Remedy Application

The success of a remedy application in public procurement often depends not on whether "we are right", but on whether it is correctly drafted, because it can only be assessed on its merits if it meets the substantive and formal requirements prescribed by law. The provisions of the Public Procurement Act are also applicable to formal compliance. It is worth reviewing the guidelines of the Public Procurement Authority. If the case is brought before the Public Procurement Arbitration Board, structured submission is particularly important. Before the Arbitration Board, inaccuracies can easily lead to formal errors. This includes, among other things, the precise identification of the infringement, a detailed description of the relevant procedural actions, legal argumentation, and proof of the applicant's legal interest. In certain cases, other authorities may also be involved. An incomplete or inaccurate application may be rejected on formal grounds, which is why it is critically important that the document is professionally prepared and well-structured.
A properly prepared application is not only legally correct but also clear, consistent and easy to interpret for the decision-maker.

Preparation and Strategic Aspects of a Dispute Resolution Request

Dispute resolution is in many cases a strategic tool, not merely a legal step. It provides an opportunity for the parties to clarify disputed issues before a formal remedy procedure, and to avoid time-consuming and costly remedy proceedings. When preparing a dispute resolution request, it is important to precisely document the contested circumstances, properly reference the legal framework, and consider whether dispute resolution is suitable for remedying the infringement without initiating a formal remedy procedure. A properly prepared request is not only legally correct but also clear, consistent and easy to interpret for the decision-maker.

Expert Consulting in Remedy Procedures

Legal and procedural questions are complex, which is why expert consulting plays a key role in developing the appropriate strategy. The consultant's task is not only document preparation but also assessing procedural risks, systematising legal arguments and determining the optimal course of action.
Proper professional support not only provides legal certainty but can also save time and money.

Legal Remedy Guide

A dispute resolution request is worth submitting when the tenderer or the contracting authority identifies a legal infringement or procedural issue that can be clarified before initiating a formal remedy procedure. This may include, for example, contradictory documentation or formal deficiencies that can be corrected through proper consultation.

In theory, a remedy procedure can be initiated without legal representation, but in practice this carries significant risk. Without precise knowledge of the legal and procedural rules, there is a substantial risk that the application will be rejected on formal or substantive grounds.

A remedy application must include the precise identification of the contested procedural action, a detailed description of the infringement, references to the relevant legislation, and proof of the applicant's legal interest. In addition, supporting evidence must be attached and the application must be submitted in the legally prescribed format.

The costs of a remedy procedure include the procedural fee, any legal representation fees, and expert costs related to preparation. These amounts can vary significantly depending on the complexity and scope of the case.
